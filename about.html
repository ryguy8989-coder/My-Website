Interests and Projects

I have a strong interest in business analytics, data-driven decision-making, and uncovering meaningful trends in complex datasets. My passion lies in using data science techniques to solve real-world problems, whether in business operations, environmental policy, or scientific research.

Recently, I have worked on projects spanning multiple domains. One of my key analyses focused on vehicle emissions, where I compared the lifetime emissions of internal combustion engine (ICE), plug-in hybrid (PHEV), and battery electric (BEV) vehicles. This project involved modeling emissions across manufacturing, fueling, and end-of-life phases, incorporating statistical adjustments for vehicle lifetime using functions like the Weibull distribution.

I also explored renewable energy adoption strategies, evaluating the trade-offs between investing in electric vehicle infrastructure and renewable energy sources based on regional budgets and energy grid compositions. This analysis aimed to optimize resource allocation for maximum environmental benefit.

Beyond environmental analytics, I have conducted image analysis in a lab setting, working with models in Python to track the movement of microscopic organisms. This involved refining models to improve accuracy in detecting small features and identify trends in behavior and expression.

Across these projects, I have developed a strong foundation in data analysis, statistical modeling, and machine learning, applying these skills to generate insights that drive strategic decision-making.
